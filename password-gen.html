Guidlines for good passwords [1]:
  • Don't reuse passwords across multiple accounts, especially for services like banking or email.
  • Consider a Password Manager (such as Bitwarder or Lastpass)
  • Otherwise:
  • Use at least 10 characters; The longer, the better.
  • If using common words or phrases, combine several.
    A four-random-words password is surprisingly strong.
  • Avoid personal details like ID, phone, name, or birthdate (yours or your family's).
  • Misspelling or adding number/symbol at the end is negligible and can be confusing.
